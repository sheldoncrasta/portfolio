🚴‍♂️ Cyclistic Bike Share Case Study – Google Data Analytics Capstone

- Understanding rider behavior to boost annual memberships 📊✨

🔎 Problem Statement
Cyclistic, a bike-share company in Chicago, serves two types of customers: casual riders who purchase single-ride or day passes, and annual members who pay for a yearly subscription. The company’s goal is to convert casual riders into annual members to increase revenue and ensure sustainable growth.

However, casual and member riders show different patterns in ride frequency, duration, and timing. Understanding these differences is crucial to target the right audience with tailored strategies.

🛠️ Solution Approach
Data Cleaning & Preparation (Using R)
  • Standardized column names and formats across multiple monthly datasets.
  • Converted date and time columns into usable formats.
  • Merged datasets into a unified dataset for analysis.
  • Handled missing values and removed inconsistent or outlier data.
Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
  • Compared trip durations and frequencies between casual riders and annual members.
  • Analyzed seasonal and weekday/weekend trends to identify peak usage periods.
  • Evaluated popular start and end stations to understand rider behavior geographically.
Visualization & Dashboards
  • Created interactive Tableau dashboards:
    1. Usage & Trends Dashboard: Shows overall ride patterns, trip durations, and user type comparisons.
    2. Geo-wise Station Popularity Dashboard: Visualizes station-wise usage to identify high-demand areas.
Business Insights & Recommendations
  • Identified key trends that differentiate casual riders from members.
  • Recommended strategies for converting casual riders into annual members: location-based promotions, weekend incentives, loyalty programs, and targeted marketing campaigns.

🌟 Key Findings
  • Trip Duration: Casual riders take longer trips on weekends → primarily leisure usage.
  • Ride Frequency: Members ride more frequently on weekdays → commuting dominates.
  • Seasonality: Peak usage occurs during summer months; tourist-heavy areas attract many casual riders.
  • Station Popularity: Some stations are under-utilized during off-peak hours → opportunities for promotions or incentives.
